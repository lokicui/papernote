One paragraph This paper gives an approach to automatic extraction of a series of sentences describing the progress of an event over time from plain text sources. Three (or more) strong points about the paper (Please be precise and explicit; clearly explain the value and nature of the contribution). (1) The paper explains the problem and the approach reasonably clearly (but see comments below). (2) The paper tackles and interesting problem. (3) The approach looks plausible.
Three (or more) weak points about the paper (Please indicate clearly whether the paper has any mistakes, missing related work, or results that cannot be considered a contribution; write it so that the authors can understand what are seen as negative aspect (1) The approach combines known methods. (2) The results, despite being a little better than known approaches, are not good (low precision and recall). (3) The english expression is poor in quite a few places (should get a native speaker to edit the paper before publication (if accepted).
Relevant for APWEB Yes
Significance Improvement over existing work
Technical Depth and Quality of Content Syntactically complete but with limited contribution
Detailed Evaluation (Contribution, Pros/Cons, Errors); please number each point The paper aims to extract sentences that summarize the progress of an event (or, rather, the news coverage of an event) over time. Sentences are selected using a combination of text analysis and machine learning methods.

How useful a timeline summary of the kind being extracted in this work would be, and how effectively it would capture the "flow" of an event is not clear. We will assume that it might be useful ...

A major problem is that aspects of the approach are not explained with enough detail or sufficiently well-justified. For example, how is a value for the "transfer factor" alpha determined? Why was the Jaccard similarity selected for use in determining the novelty weight (when other similarity methods could have been used)? If the method was to be put into practice, how would the selection of documents and the required pre-processing be achieved?

Ultimately, the method is evaluated on one (admittedly standard) data set using manual labelling of the data. The results are slightly better than some other approaches to the problem, but all methods give especially poor results (very low recall and precision), leading one to conclude that this approach has not made major progress towards solving the problem.
